# Scenario: portrait

Use for a single person or creature when the face, expression, head, shoulders, or upper body is the main visual subject.

## Analysis

Determine:

- one primary subject and any visible age cues without inventing an exact age;
- facial structure, skin or surface details, hair, eyes, expression, and gaze;
- head angle, shoulder angle, posture, and hand placement when visible;
- shot size and crop;
- camera height and lens look;
- key light, fill, rim light, reflections, and shadow direction;
- background distance, texture, and visual competition;
- intended medium: photograph, illustration, painting, 3D, or another style.

## Construction rules

1. Keep one clear focal subject.
2. Put identity, expression, gaze, and pose before decorative background details.
3. Describe visible asymmetry and distinguishing features rather than generic beauty terms.
4. Match detail to the crop. Do not describe shoes or full-body pose in a close portrait.
5. Describe light by direction and effect on the face or material.
6. Use lens and depth-of-field language only when it supports the requested visual result.
7. Keep the background subordinate unless the user explicitly asks for an environmental portrait.

## Good pattern

`single subject + expression and gaze + visible appearance + head/shoulder pose + crop and angle + light behavior + restrained background + medium/style`

## Bad pattern

`masterpiece, beautiful person, perfect face, 8k, detailed eyes, cinematic, best quality`

The bad pattern contains praise but does not define identity, expression, crop, light, or composition.

## Self-check

- Exactly one primary portrait subject is described.
- Expression, gaze, crop, and head pose are explicit.
- Detail matches what the camera can see.
- Lighting describes direction and visible effect.
- Background details do not overpower the subject.
