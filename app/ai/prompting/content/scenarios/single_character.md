# Scenario: single_character

Use for one primary person or creature when the full character design, clothing, equipment, silhouette, pose, and relationship to the environment matter more than a portrait close-up.

## Analysis

Determine:

- one primary character, their role, visible age cues, and defining physical traits without inventing an exact age;
- full-body or three-quarter silhouette, body proportions, stance, gesture, gaze, and direction of movement;
- clothing layers, footwear, equipment, accessories, materials, wear, and practical function;
- which item belongs to which body part or hand;
- camera height, view angle, crop, and enough clear space around the silhouette;
- foreground, ground contact, background depth, and the character's explicit spatial relationship to the environment;
- key, fill, rim, practical, and environmental light and their visible effects on the character;
- intended medium: photograph, concept art, illustration, painting, 3D, or another style.

## Construction rules

1. Keep exactly one primary character. Do not add companions, crowds, reflections that read as extra characters, or duplicated body parts unless explicitly requested.
2. State the character's role and readable silhouette before small costume decoration.
3. Assign clothing, equipment, and accessories to explicit body locations. Keep left/right ownership consistent when it matters.
4. Describe a physically plausible stance or action, including foot placement and ground contact for a full-body view.
5. Preserve the requested crop. Do not hide footwear or lower-body design in a full-body character task.
6. Translate abstract traits such as practical, confident, mysterious, ceremonial, or battle-worn into visible posture, materials, palette, light, and costume choices.
7. Keep environment details subordinate but spatially connected to the character through terrain, weather, light, footprints, contact shadows, or interaction.
8. Use camera and lens language to protect silhouette readability rather than to imitate a portrait close-up.
9. Avoid lists of disconnected costume tokens. Describe coherent layers, materials, fastening, wear, and function.
10. Do not imply multiple independent characters through conflicting poses, costumes, or actions.

## Good pattern

`single character identity and role + readable full-body silhouette + coherent clothing and equipment + stance or action + ground contact + camera and crop + motivated light + subordinate environment + medium and mood`

## Bad pattern

`masterpiece, beautiful ranger, detailed outfit, epic pose, cinematic forest, 8k, best quality`

The bad pattern praises the result but does not define one character, costume ownership, silhouette, stance, environment relationship, or visible light behaviour.

## Self-check

- Exactly one primary character is described.
- Role, silhouette, pose, gaze, and action are mutually consistent.
- Clothing and equipment form a coherent design and have unambiguous ownership.
- The requested full-body or three-quarter crop remains visible.
- Feet, terrain, contact shadow, and environment relationship are physically plausible.
- Lighting describes direction and visible effects without obscuring the silhouette.
